8 Best Toddler Car Seats of 2024

September 11, 2024

Of all of the products and gear you need to buy as a parent for your child, car seats rank high in terms of importance. Put simply, car seats keep your child safe—yet there seems to be an unlimited amount of options on the market, each with their own set of unique offerings, which can make the process of choosing the right one for your toddler overwhelming, to say the least.

A toddler car seat, also known as a convertible car seat, is a type of child safety seat that is designed to accommodate toddler-aged children, typically between ages 1 and 3, in both rear and forward-facing positions. These kinds of car seats usually come into play once your child has outgrown their infant car seat, but are not yet ready for a booster seat, which usually becomes useful around ages 5+.

Toddler car seats often feature a convertible design, meaning they can be used in rear and forward-facing directions and accommodate your child through various height and weight changes. These car seats also come with adjustable harness straps, multiple recline positions and removable inserts and padding that you can use to provide additional comfort for your child.

How to maintain and clean a toddler car seat

Maintaining and cleaning a toddler car seat isn’t only about organization and peace of mind, it can also help ensure the safety, durability and overall longevity of your car seat. You first want to read the car seat’s manufacturer’s manual, which usually has guidance on how to clean and maintain the seat. Regularly inspecting the car seat for any signs of wear, damage or loose parts is also important. Check the harness straps, buckles, padding, and plastic components for any cracks, tears or fraying. If your toddler spills food or drinks on the seat, try your best to clean up the mess stat to prevent the stains from setting in and potentially hardening.

When cleaning your toddler’s car seat, try to avoid harsh chemicals or bleach, which can damage the fabric, padding and plastic components. Stick to mild soap and water or gentle upholstery cleaners recommended by the manufacturer. When possible, air dry to avoid damaging the plastic and fabric. Lastly, keep an eye on the expiration date, which typically lasts 6-10 years.

Installation and setup guide

Follow these tips and suggestions for how to install a toddler car seat:

Read the manual

Start by reading the manufacturer's manual included with the car seat, which will provide specific instructions for installing and setting up the car seat correctly, in addition to important safety information.

Select installation method

Depending on your car seat and car, you may opt to use the vehicle's seat belt or the LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) system to install the car seat. Choose whichever method the car seat recommends in their manual.

Position the car seat

After placing the car seat in the location you’ve chosen in your vehicle, make sure it’s positioned securely and leveled. Make sure that the recline angle is correctly placed (often there’s a bubble indicator) for your child’s age and size.

Install the car seat

Following your manual’s instructions, thread the seat belt through the designated belt path on the car seat or attach the lower anchor connectors to the vehicle's lower anchors, ensuring a tight and secure installation.

Secure the harness

Now it’s time to buckle your child into the car seat. Adjust the harness straps to fit snugly over their shoulders and make sure it’s positioned at, or just slightly above, your child's shoulders for rear-facing mode or at or slightly below the shoulders for forward-facing mode. The chest clip should be positioned at armpit level.

Tighten the straps

Make sure that you pull the harness straps snugly to remove any slack and ensure a secure fit. You shouldn’t be able to pinch any excess material at the shoulders.

Check for movement

Make sure that the car seat is not moving freely from side to side or front to back.

Secure the top tether (if applicable)

If using a forward-facing car seat and your vehicle is equipped with top tether anchors, attach the top tether strap to the designated anchor point and tighten it securely. The top tether helps prevent forward movement of the car seat in the event of a crash.

Register the car seat

Finally, be sure to register your toddler car seat with the manufacturer. This is often a small card that comes with your car seat. Sending it in will make sure you’re informed should there be any important safety updates and recalls.

What to consider when buying a toddler car seat

Since the very basic job of this product is to protect your child in the case of an accident, it’s vital that you choose your toddler car seat wisely, in addition to other baby and toddler products like car seat strollers combos, jogging strollers, and more. Here are a few pointers that can help guide you towards the right purchase.

Strong safety features

Safety is number one when it comes to buying a car seat. All car seats on the market are required to meet or exceed the safety standards set by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) and should include energy-absorbing foam, side-impact protection, in addition to a five-point harness system.

Range of size and weight limits

Gary Kirkilas, D.O., pediatrician and spokesperson for the American Academy of Pediatrics says that the safest toddler car seats are the ones that fit the weight and height of your child. So make sure that your child fits the size and weight limits set by the car seat to ensure proper safety. Most convertible and toddler car seats accommodate up to 50 pounds when in the rear-facing position and about 65 pounds when in a forward-facing position. (See our guide on when to switch a car seat for more.) This, however, can vary between brands, so be sure to do your research carefully.

Easy installation

Installation doesn’t come easy for most parents, so try to find a car seat that outlines easy-to-follow instructions for putting the car seat safely in your vehicle. Also, consider whether you would prefer a car seat that you can install using your car’s seat belt, which is the easiest method, or using the LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) system, which uses lower anchors and top tether anchors that already exist in your vehicle, designed to connect with corresponding attachment points on child car seats.

Budget

As a big-ticket baby gear item, car seats can run the gamut in price, typically ranging between $50 and $500. For many families, cost is an important factor, so it’s important to consider your budget when purchasing a car seat, keeping in mind that more advanced features may be associated with car seats that come with higher price tags.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Which car seat is safest for a toddler?

When it comes to toddlers, Dr. Kirkilas, points out that the safest is first and foremost the one that fits their height and weight. “This is important because toddlers are rapidly growing so they will transition from a rear facing to forward facing positioning car seat,” he adds. The next safety measure he recommends looking for is a car seat that safely restrains the toddler in the car seat, ideally with an adjustable five-point harness to keep the child safely restrained should a vehicle accident occur. Lastly, he urges parents to become familiar with how to properly secure the car seat to the vehicle’s seat. “Many vehicles today are equipped with the LATCH system which are metal anchor points located in the backseats between the seat cushions,” he says. “Both the LATCH and seat belts are equally safe although the LATCH system tends to be easier to use.”

How old should my child ride in a rear-facing car seat?

While each individual car seat model comes with a maximum height and weight limitation that can help you determine when to transition them to forward-facing, the average age for this switch is between two and three, according to Dr. Kirkilas. “A rear-facing car seat is the safest positioning for a child during a vehicle accident so it’s best to keep them in this positioning as long as possible, until the child reaches the maximum height and weight limitation,” he adds.

What car seat does my 3-year-old need?

Weight and height is the deciding factor for choosing a car seat, rather than a child’s age. That said, since many three-year-olds will be of height and weight that no longer allows them to safely sit in a rear-facing car seat, many will transition to a forward-facing car seat, according to Dr. Kirkilas. “These forward-facing car seats must include a five-point harness along with a top tether and either a seat belt or LATCH system anchor system to keep the car seat in place,” he adds.

What are the different types of car seats for a toddler?

“Parents can choose from purchasing a standalone rear-facing car seat and then transition to a forward-facing car seat once they reach the height and weight maximum,” says Dr. Kirkilas. “Another option is to purchase a convertible car seat, which is a car seat that converts from a rear-facing to forward-facing positioning.” Another option that he recommends is to purchase a combination car seat which includes a booster seat for once the child is school-aged.
